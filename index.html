Engineering Philosophy

For over half my life, I have lived with constant tinnitus and unilateral hearing loss. When you have a broken sensory input that medicine can't fix, you naturally turn to engineering to force a solution.

My early work focused on mechanical exoskeletons—imposing mechanical will on biology. But true innovation requires learning biology's language. Inspired by geometric math and avian biology, I shifted to bio-inspired robotics, proving that complex locomotion and flight control come from kinematic geometry and adaptive learning rather than rigid actuators.
